Bachelor of Science in
Information Technology

College of Computer Studies

A BSIT degree is a four-year undergraduate degree program that focuses on the study of computer utilization and computer software to plan, install, customize, operate, manage, administer, and maintain information technology infrastructure . Information technology (IT) is the use of computers, software, networks, and other devices to create, store, process, and communicate information . IT is essential for various fields and industries, such as business, education, health care, entertainment, and government .

The BSIT degree program typically consists of general education courses, core courses, and elective courses. The general education courses are designed to provide students with a broad and well-rounded knowledge of various disciplines, such as mathematics, natural sciences, social sciences, humanities, and languages . The core courses are the fundamental and essential courses that cover the basic concepts and skills of IT, such as programming, data structures, algorithms, operating systems, databases, networks, security, and software engineering . The elective courses are the courses that allow students to specialize in a specific area of interest or career path within IT, such as web development, mobile development, network administration, cloud computing, artificial intelligence, or cybersecurity .

The BSIT degree program aims to prepare students to become IT professionals who can apply their knowledge and skills to solve real-world problems and meet the needs and demands of various organizations and users
